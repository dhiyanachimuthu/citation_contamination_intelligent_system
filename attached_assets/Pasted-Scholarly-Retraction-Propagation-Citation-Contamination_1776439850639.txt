Scholarly Retraction Propagation & Citation Contamination Intelligence System
1. 🎯 ABSTRACT

Scientific literature is treated as reliable even after some papers are retracted. Current academic databases mark retractions at the source level but fail to propagate this invalidation across citation networks. As a result, downstream papers continue to cite and rely on invalid findings.

This project builds a graph-based forensic intelligence system that models scientific literature as a directed citation network and computes how retracted research propagates through academic influence chains.

The system:

Detects retracted papers
Builds forward citation graphs
Analyzes propagation across 1–3 citation hops
Computes contamination risk scores
Visualizes citation spread
Identifies high-risk scientific reviews and meta-analyses
2. ❗ PROBLEM STATEMENT
2.1 Core Problem

When a scientific paper is retracted:

The retraction is applied only to the original paper
Citation databases do NOT update downstream papers
Papers that cite the retracted work remain unflagged
2.2 Critical Failure in Current Systems

Existing systems:

PubMed → marks retraction only at source level
Google Scholar → inconsistent retraction visibility
Semantic Scholar → no propagation logic
OpenCitations → provides citation links but no risk modeling
2.3 Research Gap

No system:

treats citations as a contamination propagation graph
computes downstream risk from retracted literature
ranks papers based on inherited invalidity

Built using:

NetworkX
6.1 Graph Definition
Nodes → papers (DOI-based)
Edges → citation relationship

Directed edge:

A → B means A cites B
6.2 Graph Expansion Strategy

For each retracted paper:

Level 0: Retraction source
Level 1: Direct citations
Level 2: Citations of Level 1
Level 3: Deep propagation

Limit:

Maximum 150–300 nodes per graph
6.3 BFS Traversal

Used for:

propagation discovery

7. ⚠️ CONTAMINATION RISK MODEL
7.1 Base Propagation Weights
Level	Weight
1	1.0
2	0.5
3	0.2
7.2 Influence Factor

Based on citation count:

influence = log(1 + citation_count)
7.3 Citation Context Analysis

Using:

SciBERT

Classification:

Endorsing
Neutral
Critiquing
7.4 Sentiment Weighting
Type	Weight
Endorsing	1.5
Neutral	1.0
Critiquing	0.1
7.5 Final Risk Formula
risk_score =
depth_weight × sentiment_weight × log(1 + citation_count)
7.6 Field Velocity Adjustment

Different fields propagate knowledge differently:

Medicine → high velocity
Biology → medium
Mathematics → low
